i wish it could be this time next Thursday,
live within a coin flip - live within the closing of a curtain,
in the lamina of faith on the eighth floor,
from the balcony next to the room i was born in and 8am has never been so distant;

i will never not be at that party again,
in that classroom again,
every time i close my eyes to take me there again,
to where I was when i was younger and everything was so far away;

i don’t want to bury my grandparents
- if i see a shovel i swear i will bite -
to be an adult and grow up,
to smoke more often and alone,
not hear the stories of days that died with him and her so soon;

promise you won’t disappear if i accept this,
tell me that you won’t hold yourself to it,
trust in me and peace will come,
the music will keep playing and the beaches will go nowhere -
there is a balance in the whisper of a word,
in the shaking breath that reads it and the pause which follows;
